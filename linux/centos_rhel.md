# CentOS and Red Hat Enterprise Linux

RHEL is a very popular Linux distribution. Here are some notes about support lifecycle and lineage which may be of interest to RHEL users.

## CentOS Stream

This is the upstream open-source project which RHEL is based on.
Point releases have ~6 month life cycle with planned ~10 point releases.
Support ends ~6 months following the last point release - there is No Maintenance phase like provided with RHEL.

[Stream End-of-life][1] is currently listed as the "End of RHEL * full support phase."
* Stream 9 EOL = 2027-05-31
* Stream 10 EOL = 2030-05-31

## Red Hat Enterprise Linux

[RHEL End-of-life][2]. In addition to a planned 5-year Full Support phase, the Life Cycle plan includes an 
additional 5-year Maintenance Support phase for a combined total support life of 10 years (excluding Extended Support options).
* RHEL 9 EOL = 2032-05-31
* RHEL 10 EOL = 2035-05-31

## Fedora Linux

Fedora is the upstream project which CentOS Stream and RHEL are based on. Releases are ~ every 6 months and [support is ~13 months for each release][3].

Selected [Stream and RHEL Fedora Lineage][4]
* Stream and RHEL 9 are based on Fedora 34
* Stream and RHEL 10 are based on Fedora 40
* Stream and RHEL 11 will be based on Fedora __?
